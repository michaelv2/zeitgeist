Macro

Growth & Activity

  • Industrial production essentially flat since mid-2025 (101.5 in Mar-26 vs. 101.5 in Jan-26), suggesting manufacturing is not accelerating. Pre-tariff front-running may have boosted earlier prints.
  • Nonfarm payrolls remarkably stable — monthly adds hovering ~158,400–158,700 range since mid-2025 with no breakout in either direction. Labor market is neither heating nor cracking.
  • JOLTS job openings trending down from 7,200 (Jan-26) to 6,866 (Mar-26) — softening demand for labor, consistent with mild cooling. Not yet recessionary.
  • Retail sales data (May 14 release) apparently came in constructively enough to help push markets to records.

Inflation

  • Core CPI accelerating modestly: 328.7 (Jul-25) → 335.4 (Apr-26), roughly +0.2–0.3%/month — annualizing near 3%. Not re-accelerating aggressively, but not at target either.
  • Tariff pass-through risk is the key variable; US-China 90-day pause de-risks near-term but does not eliminate structural upward pressure.
  • Hormuz disruption (if sustained) = oil supply shock = upside CPI surprise risk in June/July data.

Rates & Yields

  • 10Y yields reportedly above 4.5% (per IBD headline). Market pricing cuts cautiously; Fed unlikely to move at the May 20-21 FOMC given core CPI still elevated and labor intact.
  • Long duration ($ZROZ) remains vulnerable while inflation runs above target and fiscal deficits persist.
  • TIPS remain an attractive hedge if Hormuz/oil supply disruption materializes.

Geopolitics

Middle East / Strait of Hormuz

  • Highest near-term risk. Ship seizure and vessel attacks confirm Iranian capability and willingness to disrupt shipping. ~20% of global oil transits Hormuz.
  • Trump-Xi alignment is a diplomatic positive but enforcement mechanism is unclear. China's cooperation is contingent and self-interested (China is Iran's largest oil buyer).
  • Trade: Oil spike risk → energy equities ($XLE, $DBC) outperform; airlines, consumer discretionary underperform on fuel cost pass-through.

Russia-Ukraine

  • Escalating Russian strikes with no ceasefire in sight. Hegseth's unilateral troop cuts in Poland contradict NATO reassurance messaging.
  • Market implication: European defense budget expansion continues — bullish $XAR, European defense names (Rheinmetall, BAE).
  • EUR may face modest pressure if EU energy costs spike (via Hormuz) simultaneously.

China-Taiwan

  • Prediction markets price a ~50% chance of China invading Taiwan before ~mid-2027. This is almost certainly moonshot bias — Xi leadership (93% probability of staying through 2026) is stable, and no immediate military indicators support imminent invasion. However, the market pricing it at ~50% signals elevated geopolitical risk premium on semiconductors, Taiwan-exposed supply chains.
  • Implication: Keep some hedge in domestic-oriented semiconductor names over TSMC-dependent plays.

Trump Political Stability

  • Prediction markets near 50/50 on Trump leaving office before ~mid-2027. Apply significant moonshot discount — impeachment requires Senate conviction (currently controlled ~55% R) and no specific near-term mechanism is evident.
  • 2028 race: Vance (36%) and Rubio (25%) lead the GOP primary; Newsom (24%) leads Democratic field. A Vance/Newsom race = continuation of tech-friendly vs. regulation-risk backdrop. Market should not price material 2028 risk until mid-2027.
  • 2026 Midterms: Prediction markets price Democrats taking the House at 78.5% probability — this is the most actionable political signal. Divided government post-Nov 2026 = legislative gridlock, limits further tax cuts or spending programs; modest negative for defense/domestic fiscal trades.
