Macro

Inflation

  • Core CPI trending upward: 327.7 (Jun-25) → 334.2 (Mar-26), consistent ~2% annualized but momentum is persistent.
  • Core PCE (Feb-26: 128.86) also grinding higher. March PCE prints Apr 30 — critical for FOMC messaging.
  • Tariff pass-through risk is the key upside inflation wildcard; $166B in refunds partially offset but structural tariff costs remain.

Rates & Yields

  • 10Y-2Y spread ~+54bps; 10Y-3M ~+55bps — both slightly steepening, curve re-normalizing but not aggressively. No recession signal imminent from curve alone.
  • FOMC Apr 28-29: Hold widely expected; market focus on Warsh hearing tone + any updated guidance on balance sheet/QT pace. Powell remains in seat until Warsh confirmed.
  • Duration risk: If Warsh signals more dovish/politically influenced Fed, real yields could drop → TIPS and gold attractive; if hawkish, front-end stays elevated.

USD

  • Trade-Weighted USD declined meaningfully: 120.43 (Apr 6) → 118.08 (Apr 17), ~2% drop in two weeks.
  • Driven by tariff uncertainty, Iran tensions, and political noise. Weakening USD is: (1) inflationary for imports, (2) positive for large-cap multinationals' foreign earnings, (3) supportive for commodities/gold/BTC.

Labor

  • Unemployment stable at 4.3% (Mar-26), range-bound 4.1–4.5% since mid-2025. Not deteriorating but not tightening either. May 8 payrolls report is next key read.

Growth

  • Q1 2026 Advance GDP (Apr 29) is the week's most important macro print — tariff front-loading likely distorted trade balance; watch for headline miss vs. private demand strength.

Geopolitics

Iran / Hormuz

  • Strait of Hormuz standoff is the single biggest near-term market risk. US Navy shooting a vessel's engine out escalates physical risk premium significantly.
  • If ceasefire collapses: oil spikes, defense names rally ($XAR), shipping/logistics disrupted, LNG/energy complex bid.
  • If deal reached: oil falls 3-5%, energy underperforms, risk-on bounce.
  • Prediction market noise on Trump removal (52% "yes" before ~late 2026) likely reflects general political chaos discount, not imminent removal. Discount this — moonshot bias evident.

China/Taiwan

  • Prediction market puts ~51% on China invading Taiwan before ~late 2026; this is classic moonshot overpricing. Xi removal before 2027 at 6.5% is more realistic baseline.
  • Key watch: US-China trade dialogue (TBD date) — any escalation in tech export controls or tariff ratcheting would hit semis ($NVDA, $AMAT) and broad QQQ disproportionately.
  • Japan's lethal weapons export shift is a direct strategic response to China threat — bullish for Japanese and allied defense chains.

Russia/Europe

  • Putin removal by Dec 2026 at ~9.5% — low but non-trivial. Bulgaria Kremlin-friendly win: modest pressure on EUR, complicates NATO cohesion, marginally positive for Russian energy flows.

2028 Elections (Political Positioning, Not Trading)

  • Top contenders: JD Vance (~39% Repub nom, ~19% general), Gavin Newsom (~27% Dem nom, ~19% general), Marco Rubio (~20% Repub nom). Fragmented Democratic field.
  • Investment relevance: A Vance/Rubio win = continuation of tariff/protectionist regime → structurally inflationary, USD pressure, domestic industrials favored. Newsom/progressive Democrat = likely reversal of energy/climate deregulation, healthcare reform risk. Too early to position but worth monitoring field consolidation.

Crypto
