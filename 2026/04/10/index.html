Macro

Rates & Inflation

  • Core CPI trending steadily upward: 326.9 (May '25) → 333.5 (Feb '26), ~+2% annualized run rate — not alarming but not falling.
  • Core PCE similarly creeping: 125.8 → 128.9 over same period, ~+2.5% annualized — above Fed target.
  • Fed Funds stuck at 3.64% since Jan '26 after series of cuts from 4.33%. Rate-cut cycle appears paused.
  • Hormuz closure = near-term oil supply shock → upside inflation risk. If crude spikes materially, re-acceleration of headline CPI is possible, complicating next FOMC.
  • Chicago Fed Financial Conditions Index tightening steadily from -0.556 (Jan) to -0.433 (Apr 3) — conditions tightening even without Fed hikes. Watch for accelerating tightening if oil stays elevated.
  • Mar CPI (Apr 15) and PCE (Apr 30) are critical — any energy-driven surprise could reprice rate-cut odds sharply.

Labor

  • Unemployment stable at ~4.3-4.4% range; no deterioration signal.

  • NFP March '26 (already released Apr 3): 158,637K — flat, consistent with slow but stable job creation.
  • Net: labor not breaking down, but not hot. Fed has no urgency to cut or hike on labor alone.
