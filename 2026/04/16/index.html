Macro

Inflation

  • Core CPI rising steadily: 327.7 (Jun '25) → 334.2 (Mar '26) — persistent upward grind; no signs of re-acceleration but no decisive break lower.

  • Nonfarm payrolls remarkably flat: hovering ~158,400–158,637K since Jun '25. Essentially no net job creation over 9 months — labor market stagnation, not collapse, but a warning signal for Q2.

Financial Conditions

  • Chicago Fed FCI: Tightened from -0.552 (early Feb) → -0.458 (Apr 10) — conditions loosening less; modest tightening trend over 10 weeks. Not alarming but directionally unfavorable for risk assets if it continues.
  • USD (Broad TWI): Declining — 121.3 (Mar 30) → 118.9 (Apr 10). Dollar weakness = tailwind for multinational earnings and gold ($IAU), headwind for import inflation.

Geopolitics

  • Active naval blockade is the single biggest macro wildcard right now. Key risks:
    • Sustained oil price spike → re-accelerates CPI, freezes Fed cuts, crushes consumer discretionary.
    • Iran deal materializing → oil reverses, risk-on broadens, energy names correct.
    • Pakistan mediation appears fragile; no deal yet despite optimism.
  • Energy ($DBC, oil E&Ps) remain tactically attractive until deal is confirmed and priced.
  • Hormuz handles ~20% of global oil — even partial disruption has outsized global growth impact.

Russia-Ukraine

  • Prediction markets at ~55% for ceasefire (within ~2 years). Russia continuing heavy drone/missile strikes (18 killed Apr 15) — no imminent peace.
  • Putin removal by end-2026: only ~10% probability. Status quo war posture continues.
  • Implication: European defense spending ($XAR equivalent, European defense ETFs) remains supported. Energy (LNG alternatives to Russia) structurally bid.

China-Taiwan

  • Markets price ~50% invasion probability over a 2-year horizon — this is likely prediction market moonshot bias; actual near-term probability far lower.
  • Xi removal before 2027: only ~7% — leadership stable.

  • Watch: US-China tech/trade restrictions remain an ongoing risk to semis ($SOX, $NVDA, $AMAT).
