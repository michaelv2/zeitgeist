Macro

Energy & Inflation

  • WTI hit $114/bbl as of Apr 6 (FRED); news confirms oil climbed back above $100 on Hormuz blockade headlines — likely higher today.
  • Hormuz handles ~20% of global oil supply. A sustained blockade is a severe stagflationary shock: energy CPI spike arriving just as the Fed needs to cut.
  • Core CPI has been drifting higher: 327.7 (Jun '25) → 334.2 (Mar '26), a ~2% annualized pace — already at/above Fed comfort zone before energy passthrough.
  • Core PCE: 125.8 (May '25) → 128.9 (Feb '26), also creeping. March PCE (Apr 30) will be the next clean read.
  • Tomorrow's Mar CPI (Apr 14) is critical: pre-blockade data, but sets the baseline. Any upside surprise locks the Fed in hawkish hold mode longer.
  • Trump acknowledged gas prices may stay elevated through midterms — politically significant; removes urgency to negotiate down oil.

Rates & Yields

  • 10Y yield: 4.29% (Apr 9) — notably contained given oil spike; suggests flight-to-quality bid or growth fear offsetting inflation fear.
  • 10Y–2Y spread: +0.50–0.53% — modestly positive, curve steepening slowly; not alarming but bears watching as energy inflation reprices the short end.
  • Chicago Fed Financial Conditions Index: -0.433 (Apr 3), tightening trend from -0.556 in Jan. Conditions tightening even before the oil shock fully registers.
  • FOMC (Apr 29–30): Fed will be trapped — oil/inflation shock argues against cuts; softening labor/growth argues for them. Expect hold with hawkish language. Any cut is off the table near-term.

Labor & Growth

  • Nonfarm payrolls essentially flat ~158,400–158,600 range for 9+ months — labor market cooling but not collapsing.
  • Unemployment: 4.3% (Mar '26), range-bound 4.1–4.5% since mid-'25. No hard landing signal yet, but no strength either.
  • Industrial production: mild uptrend, 100.97 (May '25) → 102.55 (Feb '26). Manufacturing holding but vulnerable to energy cost shock.
  • Q1 Advance GDP (Apr 29): high risk of soft print given consumer fatigue + war uncertainty. A sub-2% reading would amplify stagflation narrative.

USD & FX

  • Broad trade-weighted USD: ~120.5 (Apr 3) — relatively stable but war/oil shock could drive safe-haven bid or, conversely, weaken if stagflation undermines Fed rate path.
  • JPY carry trade risk: BoJ meeting Apr 27–28. If BoJ signals further normalization while Fed holds, yen strengthens — unwinds carry trades, pressures risk assets.
  • EUR: Orbán defeat removes a key EU spoiler; modestly EUR-positive for political stability, but energy shock from Hormuz hits Europe harder than U.S.

Geopolitics

  • Dominant risk. Blockade = oil supply shock, shipping insurance spike, tanker rerouting (doubling voyage times around Cape of Good Hope).
  • Ceasefire reportedly fragile; mediators active. Markets pricing some mean-reversion probability — but any escalation (Iran mining Hormuz, proxy attacks on tankers) sends WTI toward $130–$140.
  • Second-order: LNG prices surge → European energy crisis redux → EUR weakness, German industrial stress.
  • Defense names ($XAR) rally on prolonged conflict; shipping/tanker stocks benefit; airlines, consumer discretionary, and any energy-intensive industrials are hurt.
  • Prediction markets (~53%) imply Russia-Ukraine ceasefire before mid-2027 — if that resolves, energy could partially normalize, but Iran shock dominates near term.

China / Taiwan
