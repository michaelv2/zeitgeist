Key News

  • Iran Diplomacy: Rubio hosting Israeli and Lebanese ambassadors for ceasefire talks; oil prices dipped and Treasuries rallied on peace deal optimism — fragile, watch closely.
  • Orbán Defeated in Hungary: Opposition leader Péter Magyar wins Hungarian election — signals weakening of Eurosceptic/pro-Russia bloc in Central Europe; modest EUR-positive signal.
  • China Export Slowdown: China March exports +2.5% YoY, sharply below consensus; imports surged. Middle East turmoil cited as demand headwind. Oil/gas imports from Persian Gulf compressed.
  • Evergrande Founder Guilty Plea: Hui Ka-yan pleads guilty to fraud in Shenzhen — final chapter of China's property crisis, marginal negative for Chinese property credit but not a new shock.
  • DRAM/RAM Shortage Escalating: Microsoft raises Surface prices $200–300; Apple pulling high-end Mac mini/Mac Studio from sale. RAM shortage (dubbed "RAMageddon") is a live supply chain disruption affecting consumer electronics broadly.
  • Sam Altman Targeted: Two suspects arrested after shooting at OpenAI CEO's home; prior Molotov cocktail attack. AI-related political violence a new tail risk for sector narrative.
  • Artemis II Returns: NASA crew safely returned; Artemis III/IV planning ongoing — SpaceX Starship and Blue Origin competing for Artemis IV lunar lander contract.

Macro

Inflation

  • Core CPI has been rising steadily: 327.7 (Jun-25) → 334.2 (Mar-26), tracking ~2.0–2.2% annualized — still above Fed comfort on a rolling basis.
  • Core PCE: 125.8 (May-25) → 128.9 (Feb-26) — similarly persistent ~2.5% YoY trend.
  • Key risk: Hormuz blockade is inflationary. Oil supply disruption → higher energy CPI/PPI with 4–6 week lag. Tariff pass-through and RAM shortage add further goods inflation. March and April CPI prints likely to be hotter.

Rates & Yields

  • 10Y–2Y spread holding tightly at ~+50–52bps through April 13 — curve steepening stalled, not yet pricing a rate-cut acceleration.
  • Treasuries rallied Monday on Iran peace hopes, but the blockade base case remains in effect. A durable peace deal = rates down, oil down, risk-on; escalation = stagflation risk.
  • FOMC April 29 meeting: Fed unlikely to cut into an active oil shock + sticky core inflation. Market should not expect a dovish pivot here.

Labor

  • Continuing claims recently declined to ~1.794M (Mar-28) — modest tightening signal at the margin.
  • JOLTS Feb: 6,882K openings — down meaningfully from 7,240K in Jan; labor demand softening without collapse.

Consumer & Housing

Industrial Production

Geopolitics

Middle East / Iran Blockade

  • Most impactful near-term event. U.S. blockade of Iranian ports — shadow fleet attempting to circumvent. China is the primary buyer of sanctioned Iranian crude; this is a direct escalation of U.S.-China economic conflict by proxy.
  • Second-order: China's export slowdown will deepen if Persian Gulf trade routes remain disrupted. European energy prices at risk. LNG/tanker freight rates likely to spike.
  • Oil fell on ceasefire talk but the blockade is still in force — oil directionally higher on sustained disruption. $DBC and energy ETFs remain relevant hedges.

China / Taiwan

  • Prediction market at ~51.5% for China invading Taiwan before ~mid-2027 — likely moonshot-biased but non-trivial. Xi departure before 2027 only 7.5% — Xi firmly in control.
  • China condemning Hormuz blockade signals continued geopolitical alignment with Iran; deepens U.S.-China cold war dynamic. Risk for semis (NVDA, AMAT, LRCX) and EM exposure.
  • Evergrande fraud guilty plea closes the book on property giant's downfall — no new systemic risk, but China's property sector structurally weak.

Russia / Europe

  • Putin removal by end-2026: only 9.5% probability — no leadership change expected.
  • Orbán's defeat in Hungary removes a key Russian ally in the EU/NATO — positive for European cohesion, mild positive for EUR and NATO-linked defense spending.
