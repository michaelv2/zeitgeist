Geopolitics

Iran / Strait of Hormuz

  • Most pressing near-term risk. Strait closure (even partial/intermittent) = sustained oil price pressure, shipping disruption, insurance premium spikes.
  • Second-order: LNG/energy shipping rerouted via Cape of Good Hope adds ~2+ weeks transit, tightening global supply further.
  • Third-order: Poor/import-dependent nations (South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa) face EM currency stress and potential sovereign credit events — could produce contagion to EM ETFs (EEM, EWZ).
  • Beneficiaries: U.S. energy producers (XLE, XOP), defense (XAR), tanker companies with exposure outside Hormuz.
  • Losers: Airlines, consumer discretionary, logistics, EM bonds.

Russia-Ukraine

  • Prediction markets ~53.5% for ceasefire before ~mid-2027. Talks appear stalled; no imminent resolution.
  • Putin removal by end-2026: only ~9.5% probability — continuity assumption holds.
  • Ongoing war keeps European defense spending elevated (NATO tailwind for XAR) and suppresses European growth.

China / Taiwan

  • Xi removal before 2027: only 6.7% — leadership continuity is baseline.
  • China-Taiwan invasion priced at ~50.5% on a long (multi-year) horizon — treat as a tail risk, not base case for 2026.
  • North Korean missile tests suggest coordinated axis posturing; watch for U.S. force repositioning to Indo-Pacific.
