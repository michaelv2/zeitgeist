Geopolitics

US-Iran War

  • The dominant market risk right now. Prediction markets (~51%) and news flow both point to potential ground operation — if executed, oil could spike further and risk-off accelerates.
  • Kharg Island handles ~90% of Iranian crude exports; any seizure/destruction = structural supply disruption, not just temporary.
  • Houthi missile attacks on Israel = regional contagion risk; Strait of Hormuz disruption tail risk non-trivial.
  • Second-order: Defense stocks ($XAR) benefit; airlines, trucking, consumer discretionary hurt; emerging markets with energy import dependency (especially Asia) under pressure.

Russia-Ukraine Ceasefire

  • Markets at ~54% probability of ceasefire before late 2027. Russian tanker/Cuba episode suggests Trump is open to selective dealmaking with Moscow. A ceasefire would be a meaningful European equity and EUR tailwind; reduces pressure on European energy prices (though Iran now dominates that narrative).

China-Taiwan

  • Prediction market at 51.5% for invasion before late 2027 — classic moonshot bias inflating this. Xi removal probability only 8.4% before 2027 — leadership stable.
  • With US forces tied up in Middle East (50K+ troops), China's window of opportunity is objectively wider. This is a low-probability but high-impact tail risk worth hedging via long semiconductors (TSMC-dependent supply chain) put protection.
  • Near-term: US-China trade tensions likely continue regardless.

2028 US Presidential Race

  • JD Vance (18%) and Gavin Newsom (17%) lead in general election odds — essentially a coin flip between them. Newsom at 24% for Dem nomination.
  • Policy implication: A Vance win = continuity of Trump-era tariffs/energy policy; Newsom = reversal of deregulation, likely green energy push. Too early to trade but sets the framework.
  • "Trump out before late 2027" at 53.5% — elevated but reflects constitutional/political uncertainties rather than near-term probability; treat as noise unless SCOTUS/Congress developments accelerate.
