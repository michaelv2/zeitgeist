Macro & Cross-Asset

Inflation & Fed Policy

  • Core CPI and Core PCE: Both continue a slow upward trend through Feb 2026 (latest CPI Core: 333.5 vs. Jan 332.8; PCE Core Jan: 128.4), pointing to persistent (not accelerating but sticky) services inflation. Inflation risk may rise if energy shock persists.
  • Rates/Curve: The Fed funds rate drifted downwards this year (from 4.3% to 3.64%). Recent volatility in 2Y (3.56–3.79%) and 10Y Treasury yields (4.12–4.28%) leaves the yield curve still positively sloped (10Y–2Y ~0.5%)—no hard signal of recession.
  • Financial conditions: Remain very loose (Chicago Fed Financial Conditions Index ~ -0.5), implying risky assets supported unless risks surge.
  • Dollar: The trade-weighted USD index is climbing again (now 120.6), in part on safe-haven demand; could add further pressure to EMs and US global corporates.

Real Economy & Labor

  • Labor: Nonfarm payrolls are stable (~158.5m), but job openings are trending down (from 7.3m in mid-’25 to 6.95m in Jan). Unemployment stuck at 4.3–4.4%. No evidence of a labor overheating, but not unraveling. Quit/continuing claims ticking up moderately.
  • Retail Sales/Consumer: Headline retail sales are soft, plateauing since Nov. Michigan Consumer Sentiment recently bounced (Jan 56.4 from Dec 52.9), but still at recessionary levels historically.
  • Industrial Production: Marginally up in Jan/Feb but no strong momentum.

Geopolitics, Energy, Defense

  • Middle East Risk: The Iran conflict is materially escalating. Missile attacks and explicit threats to regional oil infrastructure (and shipping via Hormuz) drive renewed energy supply risk and volatility.
    • Oil prices surged from ~$75 to nearly $100 over past two weeks before correcting (~$93). Persistent risk premium expected.
    • Upward oil price shock will transmit to CPI, headline inflation, and real incomes; could blunt consumer/retail, drive defensive sector rotation, and bolster energy equities and commodities hedges.
    • US defense spending outlook reinforced; contractors have near-term demand tailwinds.
  • Russia/Ukraine: Ceasefire prospects discussed but no fresh action or progress visible. Expect defense spending/energy price risk to persist; no collapse in Russian political leadership likely this year.
  • China/Taiwan: Market sees increased but not overwhelming risk of major action. China’s 'stability' messaging hints at focus on economic ties, not imminent conflict—but headlines keep semis/defense risk premium alive.

  • Altcoins/Airdrops: Little real money chasing moonshot airdrop/mega cap narratives outside core crypto, suggests low spillover to broader risk assets.

  • Homebuilders/REITs: Housing starts have rebounded for now, and Case-Shiller shows slight home price gains into year-end, but outlook vulnerable if rates or headline inflation re-accelerate.
  • Staples/necessities: Price spikes (tariffs, inflation) in daily goods support more resilient performance for consumer staples than for lower-income discretionary retail.

Healthcare & Regulation
