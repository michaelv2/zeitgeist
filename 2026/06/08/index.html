Key Themes

  • The market has priced the next cut out of the front end — but it's trading the wrong inflation. The front end has priced easing out entirely: 2Y at 4.05% sits ~42bp above the 3.63% funds rate, a bear-flattening (10s2s 0.49→0.38 in two weeks) that says "hold-to-hike," not cut. Yet this is a headline energy scare, not a core problem — core PCE momentum is actually decelerating (0.43→0.40→0.30→0.24% MoM Jan–Apr). The cut path is on hold and contingent, not over — the market is right that the June 24–25 FOMC holds, but it's pricing the oil spike, not the core. Long-duration (ZROZ) is the wrong seat into that meeting; TIPS + energy is the cleaner expression. Confirm/refute: May CPI on June 10.
  • The AI selloff is a positioning unwind, not a fundamentals break. Worst Wall St day in months and a Korea/Taiwan "meltdown" driven by forced selling of over-owned chip names — while Nvidia simultaneously inks fresh SK Group/NAVER infrastructure deals. The new deals suggest demand is holding, but the late-July capex guide is what confirms it — so the demand-vs-multiple split is provisional, and a rising discount rate is the accelerant. The highest-multiple cohort is the vulnerable leg into late-July mega-cap earnings. Buy-the-dip is premature until the capex guide confirms.
  • The consumer headline masks a near-term spending drag. Nominal retail looks fine (+0.5% MoM Apr) but headline CPI (+0.64% Apr, +0.87% Mar) mechanically eroded April real retail — though the memo treats that headline as a transient energy distortion, so this drag reverses if oil fades. Michigan sentiment has dropped sharply to 49.8 (from 56.6 in Feb), but that's one soft survey reading against still-firm labor (JOLTS jumped to 7.62M). This is a watch-item on discretionary pending further confirmation, not an established structural squeeze.

Macro

Rates & Fed

  • Hold on June 24–25 is locked; the risk is hawkish guidance. Funds flat at ~3.63% since February after 70bp of 2025 cuts; the 2Y repricing higher is the market taking the next cut off the table. The cut path is on hold and contingent on the data, not structurally over.
  • Stay short duration. Bear-flattening + reinflation headline + a fresh oil bid all argue against ZROZ here. The curve is no longer pricing the soft landing the equity desk assumes.

Inflation

  • Two different inflations — trade them separately. Headline is whipping on energy (Mar +0.87%, Apr +0.64% MoM); core PCE is decelerating to ~2.9% annualized. The mid-May oil spike to $112 hasn't hit the CPI yet, so May CPI (Jun 10) likely prints hot on energy — a head-fake that pressures duration but shouldn't change the core trajectory.
  • TIPS (TIPZ) are the asymmetric play: positive real yields plus a free option on oil-driven headline reacceleration.

Labor

  • Still resilient, not rolling over. Payrolls +172k to 159.0M in May; unemployment steady at 4.3% for the cycle; JOLTS openings surged to 7.62M (Apr) from 6.89M; continuing claims flat ~1.78M. This resilience is why the Fed can hold — it removes the urgency to cushion the consumer.

Consumer

  • Near-term spending drag, not a structural squeeze. Retail +0.5% MoM April nominal, mechanically eroded by an energy-distorted CPI that the memo treats as transient — if oil fades, so does the drag. Sentiment at 49.8 is a sharp drop and worth watching, but it's one soft survey against still-firm labor. Skews against discretionary near-term; watch for confirmation before treating it as more.

Housing

  • First soft print to watch, not yet a trend. Case-Shiller posted its first soft monthly print in March (331.27 from 332.00) under sticky mortgage rates (10Y ~4.47%) — a datapoint to watch for a turn, not yet a roll-over. A potential headwind for homebuilders and a slow drag on the wealth effect, but no crisis.
