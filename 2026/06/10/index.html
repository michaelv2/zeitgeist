Daily Memo (10-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The easing cycle is over, and the tape is pricing out further cuts — equities haven't fully absorbed this. The Fed cut funds to 3.63% (from 4.33% last Aug), but the 2Y has ripped to 4.17% from 3.98% on May 29 — now ~50bp above effective funds. A 2Y trading above the funds rate signals the market has priced out additional cuts; the gap also reflects term premium, so it is not a clean read on hike pricing. The fuel is sticky core inflation: core CPI is running ~3.2% and core PCE ~3.8% annualized on a 3M basis, well above target, while the energy-driven headline (~7% annualized over the same window) is likely to moderate if oil eases. This is a direct hit to long-duration tech multiples and bonds. Confirm/kill: May CPI today (8:30 ET) — core >0.3% MoM cements it; <0.2% reopens the cut path.
  • The Iran shock is inflationary at the worst possible moment, removing the Fed's room to ease even if growth softens. US-Iran strikes near Hormuz are pushing WTI back up (rebounded to ~$96 on Jun 1 after a late-May plunge from $112). A sustained oil spike feeds the same CPI the Fed is already fighting — so the geopolitical premium compounds the rate problem rather than triggering the usual "Fed-to-the-rescue" reflex. Long energy and defense; this is not a buy-the-dip-because-Fed-cuts setup.
  • A stagflation-lite squeeze is quietly building under a "resilient consumer" headline. Nominal retail sales rose +0.49% MoM in April but real retail was roughly flat — marginally negative (-0.15%) deflating by headline CPI, marginally positive (+0.1%) deflating by core — too thin a read, and too dependent on the energy spike the memo calls reversible, to declare a structural income squeeze. Michigan sentiment nonetheless collapsed to 49.8 (from 56.6 in Feb). Labor is still holding (unemployment 4.3%, payrolls +172k in May), so this isn't recession yet, but the consumer's real purchasing power is eroding. Underweight discretionary.

Macro

Inflation — re-accelerating, and it's the dominant signal

  • Headline CPI is running hot on energy/food: +0.87% (Mar), +0.64% (Apr) MoM, ~7% annualized over 3 months vs the benign sub-0.2% prints of Dec-Jan — but this is energy-driven and likely to reverse if oil eases.
  • Core is the stickier concern: core CPI +0.38% MoM (Apr), ~3.2% annualized 3M; core PCE +0.24% MoM (Apr), ~3.8% annualized 3M. The wedge is the oil/tariff pass-through into headline, but core itself is not cooling fast enough.
  • Read: the disinflation trade is dead for now. TIPS (TIPZ) screen attractive vs nominals; breakevens should widen if the Iran premium sticks.

Rates & Yields — hawkish repricing underway

  • 2Y 4.17 (Jun 8) vs effective funds 3.63 → market has priced out additional cuts; the spread also reflects term premium, so hike-risk is a tail, not a base case. 10Y 4.56, curve 10s2s +40bp (still positive, no recession signal).
  • FOMC Jun 16-17 will almost certainly hold; the risk is a hawkish dot/statement acknowledging the CPI re-acceleration. Underweight long duration (ZROZ) into this.

Labor & Consumer — holding, but under pressure at the edges

  • Payrolls grinding higher (+172k May), unemployment flat 4.3%, JOLTS jumped to 7.62M (Apr). No labor break — which is why the Fed can't justify cutting into hot CPI.
  • But real retail sales were roughly flat in April (directionally dependent on whether you deflate by headline or core) and sentiment cratered to 49.8. The consumer is absorbing the inflation, not enjoying demand strength.

Geopolitics

  • Iran/Hormuz is the live wire. Base case is contained tit-for-tat keeping a $5-15 risk premium in crude; tail risk is a Hormuz disruption spiking WTI back above the May $112 high. Express via XLE/XOP and DBC; XAR (defense) on sustained conflict.
  • Taiwan: test-firing US-supplied launchers toward the mainland; Xi consolidating the China-NK axis in Pyongyang. Near-term invasion odds remain low (6.95% by end-2026 per markets — the 50% "before GTA VI" line is a meme horizon, not a 2026 signal). Not tradeable today, but a slow-building tail for semis/supply-chain.
  • Russia: Ukrainian drone strikes causing fuel shortages in Crimea — marginal added tightness to global product markets, reinforcing the energy long.

Sectors & Names

  • Energy (XLE/XOP) — overweight. Direct beneficiary of Hormuz premium plus rebounding WTI; also a hedge against the inflation that's hurting everything else.
  • Tech / QQQ — trim into the earnings gauntlet. Rate repricing compresses the highest-multiple cohort just as a rotation out of tech is underway. Catalysts: ORCL (today AMC), ADBE (Jun 11), AVGO (Jun 12), MU (Jun 24). AVGO/MU are the cleanest reads on whether AI capex is still funding the multiple.
  • Defense (XAR) — overweight on the Iran conflict and the $70B enforcement bill's fiscal direction.
  • Consumer discretionary — underweight. Real income squeeze + sentiment at 49.8.
  • AAPL — modest positive on a credible Siri/AI showing; a relative-value long within mega-cap if you must hold tech.
  • Gold (IAU) — hold as geopolitical + inflation hedge, though a strengthening USD (broad index to 120, Jun 5) and rising real yields are a headwind; size it as insurance, not a high-conviction long.

Positioning Summary

  • Overweight: energy (XLE/XOP), commodities (DBC), defense (XAR), TIPS (TIPZ).
  • Underweight: long-duration Treasuries (ZROZ), high-multiple tech (trim QQQ), consumer discretionary.
  • Hedges: gold (IAU) as a geo/inflation hedge; keep dry powder into CPI + FOMC.
  • Avoid: chasing the SpaceX-IPO-style froth and buying tech dips on a "Fed will cut" thesis — sticky core and the oil-driven CPI have taken that put off the table.
