Daily Memo (09-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The Fed's easing cycle has run into a headline-inflation re-acceleration, and the front end is quietly pricing the reversal. Headline CPI is running ~7% annualized over the last three months (326.59 Jan → 332.41 Apr) while funds sit at just 3.63% — a near-zero real policy rate into rising inflation. The tell: the 2Y at 4.17% (Jun 5) trades above funds, i.e. the tape is pricing holds-to-hikes, not cuts, under hawkish new chair Warsh. The Jun 12 CPI → Jun 16–17 FOMC sequence is the collision point and the desk's hawkish-surprise risk is under-owned.
  • But the inflation scare is mostly headline, not core — which sets up a hawkish fade, not a spiral. Core CPI is running only ~3.2% 3M annualized vs headline's ~7%; the gap is energy and tariff goods. WTI has already collapsed ~18% (112 → 91) on the Iran–Israel ceasefire, after the April CPI window. So a hot May print (consensus "4%+") may mark peak inflation panic rather than a new trend. Variant view: the 2Y backup is overshooting, and the cleaner trade is inflation protection (gold/TIPS) over a duration short. Confirm/kill: May core CPI MoM — a sub-0.3% core alongside the hot headline validates the fade; a 0.4%+ core kills it and makes Warsh's hawkish hand real.
  • Labor is the swing factor, but the signal is mixed. Payrolls averaged only ~59k/mo over the prior nine months, but reaccelerated sharply to ~190k/mo in Mar–May 2026 — labor is not clearly softening. Michigan sentiment has nonetheless cratered to 49.8 (from 56.6 in Feb). The mixed read matters: a reaccelerating jobs market weakens the "Fed can't hike" leg, raising the genuine possibility that Warsh's hawkish posture has real room to bite. Gold remains a cleaner expression of the headline-inflation / low-real-rate tension than a straightforward duration short.

Macro

Inflation & Rates

  • Inflation is re-accelerating on the headline, and it's tariff/energy-driven — core is contained. Headline ~7% 3M annualized vs core CPI ~3.2% and core PCE ~3.8% (Jan→Apr). The honest read: a goods/energy shock, not a wage-services spiral.
  • The market has unwound the cut narrative. 2Y 4.17% (Jun 5, up from 3.98% late May) now sits above 3.63% funds; 10Y 4.55%. The Fed cut ~70bp since August (4.33→3.63) and is now boxed: inflation says hold, labor says don't hike. Base case: Warsh talks hawkish, holds on Jun 17.
  • Curve flat, not re-inverting (10s2s +0.41, 10s3m +0.76) — no recession signal, consistent with the soft-but-not-collapsing growth read.

Labor & Consumer

  • Jobs reaccelerated after a weak stretch. The nine-month average of ~59k/mo was dragged down by a weak Sep–Feb period; the Mar–May 2026 prints (~190k/mo) show a clear reacceleration. At 4.3% unemployment and JOLTS openings popping to 7,618k (Apr), the labor picture is mixed-to-resilient, not clearly softening. Claims steady (~1.78M continuing).
  • Consumer spending warrants watching but is not in structural decline. April retail sales were +0.49% MoM nominal, and real spending only dips negative when deflated by the energy-distorted headline CPI; on a core-deflated basis and across the Feb–Apr trend, retail sales are flat-to-positive. Sentiment at 49.8 is genuinely weak, but the underlying retail trend is still rising. Flag discretionary as a watch item rather than a confirmed real-income squeeze.

USD & Conditions

  • Dollar firming on the rate backup (broad index 120.1, Jun 5) — mild headwind for multinationals and commodities ex-gold. Financial conditions still easy (Chicago Fed −0.49).

Geopolitics

  • Strait of Hormuz is the live tail. Ceasefire holding but the downed helicopter shows how thin it is. Keep a small energy/oil-levered hedge (XLE/DBC) — it's cheap insurance now that WTI has round-tripped to 91.
  • Taiwan tail elevated but not imminent (7% by end-2026 in markets; the inflated "before GTA VI" 50% is moonshot/long-horizon noise). Not a near-term catalyst — supportive backdrop for defense (XAR) rather than a trade trigger.

Sectors & Names

  • Gold / TIPS (IAU, TIPZ) — overweight. The cleanest expression of stagflation-lite: low/negative real policy rate, re-accelerating headline inflation, and a Fed that is constrained even with a reaccelerating labor market. Wins on either resolution of the inflation/growth tension.
  • Long-duration Treasuries (ZROZ) — underweight near-term. 10Y backing up to 4.55% on inflation; wait for the growth-scare leg (weaker jobs) before adding duration.
  • Mega-cap tech / AI (QQQ) — neutral, selective. Chip rebound and AI capex are real, but two overhangs: rising 10Y pressures the highest multiples, and the OpenAI/SpaceX/Anthropic IPO wave is a new capital sink. Apple's muted Siri reception caps the "AI re-rate everyone" thesis. Own compute/infrastructure over consumer-AI hype.
  • Defense (XAR) — modest overweight. Hormuz + Taiwan backdrop; structural, not catalyst-driven.
  • Consumer discretionary — underweight. Sentiment at 49.8 and real spending flat-to-slightly-negative on headline deflation; watch for confirmation in coming months.
  • REITs/housing — neutral-to-cautious. Home prices stalling (Case-Shiller 331.3 Mar, down from Feb) with 10Y at 4.55%.

Positioning Summary

  • Tilt: Overweight gold + TIPS; modest overweight defense and energy-as-hedge; neutral/selective mega-cap tech; underweight discretionary and long-duration Treasuries.
  • Core call: Fade the most hawkish hike-pricing but respect that cuts are off the table near-term — front end stays elevated, Warsh holds and talks tough Jun 17. The inflation pop is largely headline and energy has already rolled; with labor reaccelerating, the growth/labor side is a less clear-cut medium-term offset than it appeared.
  • Hedges: Small oil-levered position against the Hormuz tail; gold doubles as the stagflation hedge.
  • Avoid: Long duration into the CPI/FOMC sequence; rate-sensitive discretionary names exposed to potential real-income pressure.
