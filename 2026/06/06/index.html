Daily Memo (06-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The "September cut" consensus is quietly being repriced into a "no cut, maybe hike" tape — and long-duration positioning is offside. The 2Y yield (4.05% on 04-Jun, up from 3.98% on 29-May) now sits ~40bp above effective fed funds (3.63%), which mechanically prices the average path of policy higher, not lower — i.e. the market has priced cuts out and put hikes back in the distribution. This collides with hot data (headline CPI re-accelerating, "hawkish" May jobs) and a regime change at the Fed: Warsh, a known hawk, "takes the baton." The catalyst calendar still labels Sept the "first-cut window" — that framing is stale. Confirm/kill: June core CPI (Jul 14) >0.3% MoM and a higher SEP dot plot 16–17 Jun would seal the hawkish turn; a soft core print reopens the cut trade.
  • Headline inflation's scare is energy-led and may already be peaking, but core is the tell. Headline CPI jumped +0.86% MoM (Mar) and +0.64% (Apr) as the Iran-war oil spike fed through (WTI hit $112 on 19-May). Oil has since round-tripped to ~$92–96. Core ran cooler (+0.20%, +0.38%) and core PCE is steady ~+0.24%. So the headline fright is partly a reversible energy shock — but with a live Hormuz tail, you can't fully fade it.
  • Stagflation-lite under the hood: spending holds nominally while the consumer cracks. April retail sales rose +0.49% MoM but CPI rose +0.64% — real retail sales went negative. Michigan sentiment cratered to 49.8 (Apr) from 56.6 (Feb). The nominal-strength headline masks an income squeeze; this favors defensives over discretionary.

Macro

Rates & Yields

  • Fed on hold, hawkish bias — fade the cut narrative. Funds flat at ~3.63% since January after ~70bp of 2025 cuts; the front end is repricing toward hikes, not cuts. Expect no cut 16–17 Jun and an upward SEP drift.
  • Curve flattening from the front (10Y–2Y 0.38 on 05-Jun, down from 0.49) — consistent with the market pricing a Fed that stays restrictive, not one easing into growth.
  • Long-end supply risk building — debt-ceiling resolution plus August refunding point to term-premium pressure on the 10Y/30Y. Underweight long duration.

Inflation

  • Re-accelerating at the headline, firm at the core. Headline CPI 332.4 (Apr) is running hot on energy; core +0.38% MoM and core PCE +0.24% say underlying is sticky near ~3%, not breaking lower. Combined with the oil shock, this removes the Fed's cover to cut.
  • Food inflation has a fresh supply driver (screwworm cattle bans) layered on top.

Labor

  • Resilient, which is the hawkish part. Payrolls +172k (May, to 159.0M); unemployment steady 4.3% for four months; JOLTS openings jumped to 7,618k (Apr); continuing claims flat ~1.78M. No crack here means no growth-scare lever to force cuts.

Consumer & Housing

  • Real spending stalling despite nominal gains (April real retail negative); sentiment at 49.8 signals demand fragility ahead. Tilt away from discretionary.
  • Home prices rolling over — Case-Shiller slipped to 331.3 (Mar) from a Jan peak of 331.9; starts still solid (1,465k). Price stall + higher-for-longer rates = headwind for homebuilders and housing-levered names.
