Key Themes

  • The cut cycle is over; the tape is quietly pricing HIKES. Fed funds sits at 3.63% (cut from 4.33% last August), yet the 2Y yields 4.05% — ~42bp above funds, and it rose from 3.98% (May 29) to 4.08% (Jun 3) on the hot jobs print. With core CPI re-accelerating (+0.38% MoM in April after two ~0.2% months) and an oil shock layered on, the new Warsh Fed has no room to cut and a live hike tail. This is the dominant mispricing risk: long-duration ($ZROZ, $VNQ) is the wrong place to be, and the Jun 10 CPI is the trigger. Confirm/refute: core CPI MoM ≥0.3% confirms; ≤0.2% kills it.
  • Consumer is two-faced — spending real, confidence collapsing. Michigan sentiment cratered to 49.8 (from 56.6 in Feb) even as nominal retail sales rose +0.5% MoM and payrolls added +172k. Real retail sales are still modestly positive, so demand is intact today — but sentiment is a leading tell, and a sustained oil spike is exactly the catalyst that flips the squeeze from latent to actual.
  • Iran/Hormuz is a live military exchange, not a headline. US intercepting Iranian drones over Gulf states and striking radar sites; WTI whipsawed 112→91→96 in ten days. The energy bid is a hedge against both the geopolitical tail and the inflation re-acceleration — a rare two-for-one.

Macro

Rates & Yields

The whole curve is repricing hawkish. 2Y at 4.05% above a 3.63% funds rate is the market saying no more cuts, hike risk live. 10Y at 4.47%, 10Y–2Y +0.38 (flattening from +0.49), 10Y–3M +0.77 — no recession signal, just an inflation problem. Financial conditions remain loose (Chicago Fed –0.49). Underweight duration. The April FOMC minutes (mid-June) and the Jun 16–17 meeting now matter more for the tone than any action — hold is locked, but a hawkish dot/statement from Warsh repriced higher is the risk.

Inflation

Core CPI re-accelerating: April +0.38% MoM, the hottest in this run. Core PCE steadier at +0.24% MoM (~3% annualized) but not falling. Layer the oil move on top and the disinflation narrative is dead. May CPI (Jun 10) is the single most important print on the calendar.

Labor & Consumer

Payrolls grinding higher (+172k), openings re-firming, continuing claims range-bound ~1.78M — no crack. But Michigan sentiment at 49.8 is recessionary-level despite full employment; the gap says households feel the price level even as they keep spending. Resolve: retail sales is real for now (real retail sales positive), but this is the channel that breaks first if WTI holds >100.

Dollar & Commodities

Broad USD softening (118.9 from 119.5). WTI ~96 off the 112 spike but structurally bid on Hormuz. Inflationary and growth-negative if sustained.

Positioning Summary

  • Net: lean into the inflation/hawkish-rate regime — energy + TIPS over long bonds, value over long-duration growth. Keep a geopolitical hedge (gold + energy) given live Hormuz risk.
