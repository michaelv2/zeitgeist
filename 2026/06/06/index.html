Daily Memo (06-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The oil shock is real but already fading at the margin — the durable damage is to the Fed's hands, not to crude. WTI spiked to ~$112 on the US–Iran exchange of fire and Hormuz scare, then bled back to ~$92–96 as the strait stayed open and Iran's retaliation (drones at Bahrain/Kuwait, mostly intercepted) proved containable. The market is correctly fading the closure tail. The under-appreciated problem: this hits a core-inflation tape that was already re-accelerating before the spike — core CPI +0.38% MoM in April (~4.6% annualized), the hottest print in months. Layer an energy shock on top and the Fed cannot deliver the cuts the curve flirted with in May. The 2Y at ~4.05% sitting above the 3.63% funds rate is the tell: the market has quietly stopped pricing easing. June 17 FOMC will be a hawkish hold, and a hot May CPI on June 10 could force a repricing toward "higher for longer / next move could be a hike."
  • Stagflation-lite is the regime to position for, not recession. Michigan sentiment cratered to 49.8 and housing is rolling over (Case-Shiller flat-to-down three months), yet JOLTS openings jumped to 7,618k, payrolls keep grinding higher, and nominal retail is strong. That's sticky demand + sticky prices + a gloomy consumer — the worst mix for long duration and the best for real assets.
  • Trump's "government equity stake in AI" trial balloon is a new, unpriced overhang for megacap AI. Floating a US profit-share/equity stake in OpenAI and AI giants introduces political-capture risk into the highest-multiple part of the market right ahead of WWDC, MSFT, and NVDA prints. Watch for further analysis on whether this is rhetoric or a structured proposal — it reframes the AI trade if it gains legs.

Macro

Inflation — the binding constraint

Core CPI re-accelerating: 334.165 (Mar) → 335.423 (Apr), +0.38% MoM, and this predates the oil spike. Core PCE softer at +0.24% MoM (129.63 Apr) but still above target pace. The energy shock will leak into May/June headline (CPI June 10, core PCE June 26). Disinflation narrative is dead for the summer.

Rates & Yields

Funds held at ~3.63% since January after cutting 70bp through late 2025 — the Fed is paused, not easing. 2Y rose to ~4.05% (above funds), 10Y ~4.47%, 10Y–2Y +0.38 and flattening into the oil move. The belly is pricing no cuts; an equity desk fearing hikes should note the 2Y hasn't yet broken decisively higher — it's pricing "stuck," not "tightening." Underweight long duration ($ZROZ) — inflation re-accel + oil + term premium all push the wrong way.

Consumer — the squeeze

Nominal retail strong (757,085 Apr, +0.49% MoM) but Michigan sentiment at 49.8 is recessionary-low. April real retail was modestly positive, but a gasoline-inflated May print will overstate demand — read the next retail report net of energy. The consumer is spending while miserable; an oil tax at the pump is exactly what tips that.

Labor

Stable-to-firm: unemployment steady 4.3%, payrolls 159,001k (May), JOLTS openings rebounding to 7,618k, continuing claims range-bound ~1.78M. No labor crack to justify cuts — reinforces the hawkish-hold call.

Dollar & Conditions

Broad USD ~119, softening slightly despite the risk event (notable — geopolitical premium not flowing to the dollar). Chicago Fed conditions still accommodative (~-0.49). Financial conditions are not the brake; inflation is.

Geopolitics

  • Iran is the dominant near-term risk. Base case: contained tit-for-tat, Hormuz stays open, oil settles in the $85–100 band — elevated vs pre-conflict. Tail risk (real closure) would send crude toward $130+ and is the single biggest portfolio threat; the market prices it low (oil's retreat) but the thin SPR means any escalation has an outsized price response.
  • Taiwan invasion by end-2026 at ~6.6% — correctly low; ignore the 50% "before GTA VI" long-dated meme.
  • Ukraine striking St. Petersburg ("Putin's Davos"), Putin rejecting talks — war grinding, no near-term resolution, supportive of defense/energy premia.
