Daily Memo (06-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The inflation "scare" is a reversible supply shock, not a demand problem — and a hawkish Warsh Fed risks over-tightening into it. Headline CPI is running ~7% annualized (Jan 326.6 → Apr 332.4), but that is the Iran-driven oil spike (WTI hit $112 on 19-May) plus screwworm beef. Core PCE is quietly decelerating (MoM +0.40% → +0.30% → +0.24% over Feb–Apr, latest ~2.9% annualized). The front end is pricing the cutting cycle as over and hikes coming (2Y at 4.05% sits ~40bp above 3.63% funds) — that's the mispricing if oil stays sub-$95. Tell: WTI holding ≤$95 and May CPI (10-Jun) cooling would confirm the spike fades by summer.
  • The curve is bear-flattening on tightening-into-slowdown fear, not strength. 10s2s collapsed from 0.49 to 0.38 in two weeks. With a hawkish new chair and a hot headline tape, this is a stagflation-lite signal — the market fears Warsh hikes into a softening real economy, not a reflationary boom.
  • Fiscal cliff is the slow-building tailwind for gold and the headwind for the long end. The $41T debt ceiling binds late-winter/spring 2026, into a divided-government midterm (Dems ~82% to take the House, Senate a tossup). Term-premium and debt-monetization risk support gold; DXY already softening (119.4 → 118.9).

Macro

Inflation

  • Split the tape: headline hot, core benign. Headline CPI ~7% annualized (3-mo) vs core CPI ~3.2% and core PCE decelerating to ~2.9%. The wedge is energy + beef — supply, not demand. Treat the headline as transient unless the Iran ceasefire breaks.

Rates & Yields

  • Front end is pricing hikes the data doesn't justify. 2Y 4.05% vs funds 3.63%. If core PCE keeps cooling and oil stays down, the belly (3–7y) is rich-able as the hike pricing unwinds; I'd favor the belly over both wings.
  • Avoid reaching for long duration here. Fiscal supply + debt-ceiling brinkmanship cap any long-end rally even in a growth-scare; 10Y stuck ~4.47%.

Labor

  • Solid but not accelerating. Payroll level has crept ~159.0M from ~158.4M over six months — steady drift, not a boom; unemployment pinned 4.3%. Continuing claims flat ~1.78M. This is a labor market that gives the Fed no urgency to hike — reinforcing the over-tightening risk.

Consumer

  • Spending holds over the quarter but April shows the squeeze starting. Real retail sales were +~1.3% over Jan–Apr, but April alone was negative in real terms (nominal +0.49% vs CPI +0.64%). Sentiment has cratered (Michigan 49.8 vs 56.6 in Feb). The energy spike is beginning to eat real purchasing power — watch whether it compounds.

Housing

  • Stabilizing, not breaking: starts firm (1,465k Apr), Case-Shiller flat-to-down at the margin (331.3 Mar vs 332.0 Feb). Lower front-end-driven mortgage relief is off the table with the hawkish repricing.

Geopolitics

  • Iran is the swing factor for the whole tape. Ceasefire holding = oil bleeds lower = headline CPI fades = front-end hike pricing unwinds (risk-on). Ceasefire breaks = oil re-spikes = stagflation-lite, bad for both stocks and bonds. Base case: fragile hold, premium decays.
  • Taiwan risk is near-term low despite the novelty 50/50 "before [late-2026]" market — the dated market (end-2026) prices just 6.6%. Don't pay up for tail hedges here.
  • European rearmament theme intact (Hegseth D-Day rhetoric, Ukraine striking St. Petersburg) — structural support for defense.
