Daily Memo (12-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The bond market is fighting a disinflation it's about to get. The 2Y has repriced hawkishly to 4.13% — ~50bp above the 3.63% funds rate, i.e. pricing the cutting cycle as over and net-hikes as the tail. But that repricing rests on a spring headline-CPI spike that was energy-driven (headline ran ~8% annualized over the last 3 months: May 333.979 vs Feb 327.46) while core is benign, not accelerating — +0.21% MoM in May (vs +0.38% in April), though a single soft month does not yet establish a downtrend pending the Jun 26 PCE. The Iran peace framework is now collapsing oil (WTI off ~2-3 month lows, deal reopens Hormuz + lifts sanctions). If the deal holds, the headline bump reverses straight into the July 14 CPI — a dovish surprise the 2Y is positioned against. Long duration setup; tell = WTI sustained sub-90 + a soft July headline.
  • Asset euphoria vs. firm spending and collapsing confidence. SpaceX IPOs today as the largest debut ever (Musk a paper trillionaire) into a tape Barron's itself calls propped by "hopes and dreams" — while Michigan sentiment has collapsed to 49.8 (from 56.6 in Feb). Labor is stable and nominal spend is holding, but the gap between what consumers are doing and how they feel is widening; respect the froth signal but don't chase the space/IPO halo.
  • El Niño is a quietly building food/insurance risk. A "very strong" El Niño is now official just as China warns of extreme floods threatening its rice crop. Soft-commodity and P&C-cat risk that the equity tape appears to be underpricing.

Macro

Rates & Inflation

  • Hold next week, but the cut door is reopening. Funds at 3.63% (parked since January); FOMC June 16–17 in blackout now. The hot headline prints justify a hold, but they're energy, not breadth — core CPI +0.21% MoM (~2.5% annualized) and core PCE +0.24% in April (~129.63) are benign-to-sticky, not accelerating.
  • The 2Y at 4.13% is the rich asset. Above funds, it prices a hawkish-hold-to-hikes path that an oil collapse undercuts. 10Y at 4.55%, curve +40bp (10s2s) — positively sloped, no recession signal.

Labor

  • Stable, not cracking. Unemployment steady 4.3%, payrolls grinding up (159,001k May), JOLTS openings jumped to 7,618k in April. Continuing claims flat ~1.79M. Labor is not the channel forcing cuts — inflation is.

Consumer

  • Nominal demand firm, real demand modestly positive on a core-deflated basis, confidence breaking. Retail sales +0.5% MoM April (757.1B); deflating by the energy-driven headline CPI flatters the squeeze, while deflating by core (+0.21%) leaves real spend modestly positive — consistent with the memo's own call that the headline bump is a reversible energy artifact. Michigan 49.8 is the tell: the consumer is funding spend, not feeling it. Cheaper gasoline from the oil drop is the one genuine relief valve ahead.

USD & Oil

  • Dollar firm (broad index ~120, near highs); oil the swing factor. A finalized Iran deal that returns barrels + OPEC+ on June 22 is a double-bearish setup for crude. Strong USD is a near-term headwind for gold and a drag on multinational translation.

Geopolitics

  • De-escalation in the Gulf, escalation everywhere structural. Iran talks calm the oil tail; meanwhile Ukraine's drone campaign is crippling Russian logistics and Europe is in a rearmament credibility crisis (UK resignation). Net: defense demand is secular, not headline-dependent.
  • Prediction-market noise to discount: the "before GTA VI" Taiwan/Trump markets (~50%) are meme-distorted — the dated Taiwan-invasion-by-2026 market sits at 6%. The signal worth taking: 2026 midterms point to divided government (Dem House 82.5%, R Senate 55.5%) → fiscal gridlock, no fresh stimulus, recurring debt/funding friction. Reinforces the disinflation-into-2027 base case but raises long-end supply risk via the late-July Treasury refunding.

  • Defense (XAR/ITA) — overweight. "Department of War," $750M Ukraine, European underspending backlash; Iran calm doesn't dent the secular bid.

  • GOOGL over AAPL on the Siri/Gemini deal; Google monetizes the inference, Apple concedes the lead.

  • Space/IPO complex — don't chase. SpaceX debut is a sentiment event; treat the halo names as froth, not allocation.
  • Gold (IAU) — neutral. Disinflation + eventual cuts are constructive, but a firm dollar caps it near-term.
