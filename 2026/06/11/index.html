Daily Memo (11-Jun-2026)

Key Themes

  • The Fed is trapped, and the market is only half-pricing it. Powell's committee has cut ~70bp since August (funds 4.33%→3.63%) straight into inflation that remains uncomfortably elevated — headline CPI 4.2% YoY, the hottest in three years, even as MoM prints have been decelerating. The 2Y at 4.13% now sits a full ~50bp above the funds rate, so the front end has stopped pricing cuts and started sniffing a hawkish freeze. The equity-desk fear is "hikes"; the more likely reality is a Fed paralyzed — easing impossible, hiking into a war-driven oil shock politically toxic. That's stagflation-lite, and it argues for real assets over both duration and multiple. Tell: the June 17 dots and whether 2Y holds above 4%.
  • The Iran strikes are a supply-premium event, not a one-day headline. Two days of US-Iran exchanges, tankers disabled in the Gulf of Oman/near Hormuz, Iranian fire at Gulf states — WTI is bid to $95+ and the risk is asymmetric to the upside (Hormuz ≈ 20% of seaborne crude). This is the accelerant bolted onto an already-hot tariff-driven CPI. Energy and the inflation trade are the same trade right now.
  • Tech/AI is the funding source for the rotation. Shares are "extending losses" on war + rates + genuine bubble doubt (record SpaceX IPO at $135; Chanos publicly short "hopes and dreams"). The highest-multiple cohort is the most rate- and risk-sensitive leg into a hawkish FOMC. Rotate growth → real assets/defensives.

  • UK unrest (Belfast riots, Burnham by-election path) and Xi–Kim summit — second-order EUR/GBP and Asia-risk noise, not yet tradable.

Macro

Inflation — the dominant signal

  • Sticky on a YoY basis, cooling at the margin — but an oil-driven re-acceleration is the live risk. Headline MoM ran +0.87% (Mar), +0.64% (Apr), +0.47% (May) — a decelerating sequence, with core only ~0.21% MoM (~2.5–3% annualized), telling you the YoY elevation is tariffs + energy, not broad demand. The Fed can't cut its way out of a goods/oil shock without unanchoring expectations, and an Iran-driven energy spike is precisely the catalyst that could reverse the MoM deceleration and make the YoY problem worse.
  • Core PCE (Jun 26) is the swing data point. Core PCE momentum is still tamer (Apr 129.63). If it re-accelerates, the "transitory tariff pass-through" hope dies and the hawkish freeze hardens.

Rates & Yields

  • Front end has flipped hawkish; respect it. 2Y 4.13% > funds 3.63% = no more cuts priced, hike tail building. 10Y at 4.53%, curve +40bp (10s2s) — a bear-steepening bias as inflation risk premium rebuilds in the long end.
  • Avoid duration. Long Treasuries (ZROZ) are the wrong place into a sticky-inflation + oil-shock + heavy-supply setup (Treasury refunding looms in August). TIPS (TIPZ) are the cleaner rates expression.

Consumer — the income squeeze

  • Headline spending masks roughly flat-to-slightly-negative real demand. Retail sales +0.5% MoM (Apr) deflated by the CPI print leaves real retail roughly flat to marginally negative; this is price, not volume. Michigan sentiment has cratered to 49.8 (Apr) from 56.6 (Feb). Critically, the deflator is itself energy- and tariff-inflated — so the squeeze may prove as reversible as the price spike that caused it. Consumer discretionary is a tactical underweight, not a structural income-squeeze call; an oil-driven gas-price jump would tighten it further.

Labor — quietly steady, not cracking

  • No recession signal here. Payrolls grew +172k to 159.0M (May), unemployment pinned at 4.3% for months, JOLTS openings jumped to 7.62M (Apr), continuing claims flat ~1.78M. A firm labor market is precisely what prevents the Fed from cutting through hot inflation — it reinforces the freeze, not a dovish pivot.

Geopolitics

  • Iran: the tradable core is oil and defense, not a directional equity call. Long energy/defense; the tail is Hormuz disruption → oil spike → another inflation leg. OPEC+ (late June) is the next supply lever.
  • Taiwan: "before GTA VI" market at ~50% is a meme-horizon artifact; the dated market (invasion by end-2026) sits at just 6.5%. Not a 2026 base case — don't position for it.
  • Politics: House ~81% to flip Democratic, Senate leans R (55%) → divided government priced for 2027, meaning fiscal gridlock into the Sept 30 / Oct 1 funding deadlines. Trump's inflation messaging is a midterm liability, not yet a market mover.

Sectors & Names

  • Energy (XLE/XOP, DBC) — overweight. Direct beneficiary of the war premium and the inflation tape; OPEC+ late June a catalyst.
  • Gold (IAU) — overweight. Negative real funds rate (3.63% vs 4.2% CPI) + war + a Fed that can't tighten = textbook gold. Even with a firm DXY (120) as a partial offset.
  • Defense (XAR) — overweight. Iran strikes plus a Ukraine war now longer than WWI keep order books and political support firm.
  • TIPS (TIPZ) — overweight vs nominal duration. Cleanest way to own the rates view without fighting the inflation/supply backdrop.
  • Mega-cap tech / QQQ — underweight tactically. Most exposed to the hawkish-FOMC + war-vol + bubble-doubt combination. NVDA (Aug) and the July mega-cap prints (AAPL, MSFT, TSLA) are the re-rating risk windows.
  • Consumer discretionary — underweight tactically. Real demand flat-to-slightly-negative, sentiment at 49.8, gas-price headwind incoming; underweight reflects the energy/tariff price squeeze, which reverses if the price spike fades.
  • Housing — neutral/firm, watch. Starts resilient (1,465k Apr), Case-Shiller flattening (331.3 Mar). A bear-steepening 10Y at 4.53% is the threat; not yet biting.

Positioning Summary

  • Overweight: energy, commodities, gold, defense, TIPS, short-duration cash.
  • Underweight: long-duration Treasuries (ZROZ), high-multiple tech, consumer discretionary.
  • The single trade: own the inflation/oil-shock complex (energy + gold + TIPS), fund it from duration and growth multiples. The consensus "Fed will keep cutting" is stale; the front end already knows.
  • Hedge: oil-upside/Hormuz tail via energy beta; gold as the all-weather stagflation hedge.
  • Avoid: adding duration before the August refunding; chasing tech dips before June 17.

Key Tells

  • FOMC June 17 dots/statement: a pause or hawkish hold confirms the trapped-Fed call; any cut signal rebuts it and would re-rate growth higher.
  • 2Y vs funds (3.63%): 2Y holding >4% = market prices no cuts/hike tail (stay short duration); a fall back toward funds = cuts back on, soften the underweight.
  • Strait of Hormuz / WTI: sustained >$100 = supply premium is real and the inflation leg extends; a pullback to ~$90 caps it.
  • Core PCE (Jun 26): re-acceleration kills the "transitory tariff" hope; a soft core means the spike is energy-only and more contained.
  • Real (CPI-deflated) retail sales: flat-to-negative confirms the tactical income squeeze (stay UW discretionary); a genuine real gain, or a fade in the energy/tariff deflator, rebuts it.
